Basic plot > Column Chart: Total Deaths by Country

A column chart is a vertical bar chart. Each column represents the total deaths for a specific country, making it easy to discern which countries have higher or lower death counts. A Column Chart naturally facilitates ranking, as viewers can see which countries have the highest and lowest death counts by comparing the heights of the columns. Column Charts are widely used and familiar to most people, making them easy to interpret for a broad audience. Viewers can quickly understand the relationship between countries and total deaths without needing specialized knowledge or training. All this makes it effective for identifying top contributors or outliers in the data set. Its effective in comparing magnitudes, showing temporal trends, and facilitating ranking.

Variation 1 > Geo Chart: Total Deaths by Country

A geochart is a map of a country, a continent, or a region. I chose this chart as the dataset involved countries. Showing the total death count on the map provides a global perspective, allowing viewers to see how the distribution of deaths varies geographically. GeoCharts allow for easy comparison between different countries. By visually representing the data on a map, viewers can quickly identify which countries have higher or lower numbers of deaths over the specified period. GeoCharts to visualize total deaths in different countries over the years can be a powerful and effective way to communicate insights from your data, particularly if geographical distribution and temporal trends are essential aspects of the analysis.

Variation 2 > Pie Chart: Total Deaths by Country

A pie chart is a type of graph representing data in a circular form, with each slice of the circle representing a fraction or proportionate part of the whole. All slices of the pie add up to make the whole equaling 100 percent and 360 degrees. Each slice of the pie represents a country's proportion of total deaths, allowing viewers to compare the relative importance of each country at a glance. With a limited number of categories (countries in this case), a Pie Chart can provide a clear and straightforward representation of the data without overwhelming the audience with too much information. Large slices (representing high deaths) or small slices (representing low deaths) can draw viewers' attention to countries of particular interest.

Variation 3 > Area Chart: Total Deaths by Country

An area chart is a type of graph that displays data points as a series of data points connected by straight line segments and enclosed by an area. The area between the line and the x-axis is commonly filled with a color or pattern to enhance visualization. They are excellent for displaying temporal trends over a continuous interval, making them suitable for showing how the total deaths in each country have evolved from 1989 to 2022. The filled area beneath the line can effectively highlight the magnitude of deaths over time. They also provide a visually appealing representation of data while maintaining clarity and simplicity. Overall, an area chart is a suitable choice for your use case as it effectively communicates temporal trends and allows for comparison between countries' total deaths over the years. Its visual clarity, emphasis on magnitude, and interactive capabilities make it a powerful tool for conveying insights from your data to your audience.
