Rising Temperatures, Rising Concerns: Delhi's Climate Change Story

Weather Information

This dashboard provides a visualization for the weather

Plot 1: Line Plot of Average Temperature in North Indian Cities (April - June 2024): This line plot illustrates the average temperature trends in five North Indian cities—Delhi, Jaipur, Lucknow, Amritsar, and Chandigarh—over the months of April, May, and June 2024. Each city is color-coded for clarity.

Insights:

  • April: Observe the initial rise in temperatures as the summer season begins. Delhi and Jaipur typically exhibit higher average temperatures compared to other cities.
  • May: Temperatures peak in most cities, with Delhi reaching its highest. This month clearly shows the severity of summer in North India.
  • June: Although some cities might see a slight dip due to the onset of pre-monsoon showers, Delhi and Jaipur continue to record high temperatures.

Interpretation:

  • Delhi consistently shows the highest average temperatures, indicating its susceptibility to extreme summer heat.
  • The temperature trends highlight the intense heat experienced in these cities, with Delhi and Jaipur being particularly hot.

Plot 2: This stacked bar chart presents the average wind speed in the five North Indian cities for the months of April, May, and June 2024. Each city is represented by different colors in the stacked bars.

Insights:

  • April: Wind speeds are moderate, with some variability across cities.
  • May: Noticeable increase in wind speeds, especially in Delhi and Jaipur, likely due to the heat generating stronger wind currents.
  • June: Wind speeds generally increase further as the monsoon season approaches, with Lucknow and Amritsar showing significant increases.

Interpretation:

  • Wind speeds tend to rise with the increasing heat of summer, peaking just before the monsoon season.
  • Higher wind speeds in June might be due to pre-monsoon winds, which help in reducing the extreme heat temporarily.

Plot 3: This line plot explores the relationship between humidity and wind speed across the five North Indian cities over the months of April, May, and June 2024. The cities are color-coded, and the thickness of the lines represents wind speed.

Insights:

  • April: Lower humidity levels with moderate wind speeds are observed. Delhi and Jaipur have the lowest humidity.
  • May: Humidity starts to rise slightly but remains relatively low compared to June. Wind speed increases, especially in Delhi.
  • June: Significant increase in humidity as the monsoon season approaches, particularly in Lucknow and Amritsar. Wind speeds also show variability with thicker lines indicating higher speeds.

Interpretation:

  • There is an inverse relationship between humidity and temperature, especially noticeable in the transition from May to June when humidity increases and wind speed peaks before the monsoon.
  • The increased thickness of the lines in June indicates stronger wind speeds, which are typical just before the onset of the monsoon rains.
  • Humidity vs. Wind Speed: As wind speeds increase, the humidity levels tend to decrease, supporting the hypothesis of an inverse relationship.
